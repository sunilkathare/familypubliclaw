Threshold evidence

Threshold is met because:

Pursuant to section 31 of the Children Act 1989, the Local Authority submits that at the relevant date, the baby was suffering and was likely to suffer significant harm. The harm (and the likelihood of harm) was attributable to the care given to the child (or likely to have been given to the child without an order being made) not being what it would be reasonable to expect a parent give him.

The Local Authority rely on the following:

1) The respondent mother and putative father have had past experiences of neglectful and harmful parenting from their own parents. They both limited insight into the impact of their experiences on their behaviours.

2) There is a history of aggressive behaviours, drugs and alcohol use. They have not been able to make any significant changes to their lifestyles during the pre birth assessment period.

3) The respondent mother has placed herself and her unborn children in risky situations. She jumped out of a window and ended up with cuts but refused to let anyone check her or the unborn baby.

4) There have been missed antenatal appointments and failure to take necessary medication for diabetes.

5) The respondent mother has continued to use drugs and drink alcohol throughout her pregnancy. Baby was born prematurely.

6) The mother is unable to address her own care needs consistently. She is often unwashed and doesn’t take prescribed medication as instructed. As such, there are concerns about her ability to ensure a routine and basic care for the baby. The respondent mother’s accommodation is dirty and unhygienic, faeces have been smeared on the wall, clothes are not washed. There are frequent visitors, the identity of whom

7) There are ongoing concerns about violence between the parents. The father has made threats to harm the mother and unborn baby. On 6 April 2018, the respondent mother stated that the father had hit her and recently pushed her over in the street and she fell into the road.
