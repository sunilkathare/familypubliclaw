Accessibility and assisted digital

Link to presentation on accessibility and assisted digital

We had been to a training workshop run by Alistair Duggin (Head of Accessibility at GDS) and Richard Morton (Senior Accessibility Specialist at GDS). We followed up the training with two visits to the Empathy Lab at GDS. 

Now, we have a good understanding of how to design an accessible service and we also have ideas on how we’ll design the assisted digital support model for our service.

How we plan to meet the GOV.UK accessibility requirements

  • We will be applying WCAG 2.0 design principles, Perceivable, Operable, Understandable and Robust (POUR) to achieve WCAG 2.0 level AA rating.
  • We will continue to use a combination of automated tools and manual tests (including those listed in the accessibility checklist created by 18F) to identify potential problems.
  • We will be writing accessibility acceptance criteria.
  • We will budget and plan for accessibility audit with an expert in Private beta.
  • Follow inclusive design principles.
  • Test with assistive technologies including screen readers like JAWS and speech recognition software like Dragon.
  • Test with people in their own environment.
  • Test with automated tools like Sortsite.
  • Check if users can tab through the interface.
  • Check the service can be used with just keyboard.
  • Perform manual checks.
  • We will strive to learn more and we have signed up to government accessibility group.

11. Make a plan for being offline

In the event of the digital service being taken temporarily offline, users can use old paper based application form, which they are currently using.

  • View information on data recovery, resilience and disaster recovery

If the service is offline users will be shown ‘shutter’ page - there are 2 variants of the page.

Scenario 1 - offline < 15 minutes

  • Display 'shutter' page informing the users that the service is down
  • Ask users to ‘Try again in a few minutes’
  • Signpost to GOV.UK

Scenario 2 - offline > 15 minutes < 15 minutes

  • Display 'shutter' page informing the users that the service is down
  • Provide alternative ways to access the service e.g. call the contact centre for a copy of the paper form (current offline process)
  • Signpost to GOV.UK

The most likely causes for the service going offline is manual error, this will be mitigated by using automated continuous integration, testing and deployment.

13. Make the user experience consistent with GOV.UK

We have designed a service consistent with the user experience of the rest of GOV.UK including using the design patterns and style guide from the new design system. We tried different patterns and decided to use the tasklist pattern from the GOV.UK design system as it meets our users needs. We have collaboratively enganged with GOV.UK design community including:

  • Signing up for digital service designers mailing list.
  • Engaging with other designers on design channel, content design channel, prototye-kit channel, service design channel and user-research channel on the UK government digital Slack to the validate design choices, share ideas, ask for help when needed and help other when possible.
  • Sharing ideas on the GOV.UK Design System community backlog.
  • Attending events and training from the Cross-Government User-Centred Design Community. For example, atteding training on getting started with the GOV.UK prototype kit and introduction to accessibility.
  • Written posts on why we are using the tasklist design pattern. Link on the pattern backlog.

In Alpha:

  • We used GOV.UK style guide on content and patterns from the GOV.UK design system to make sure our service content is consistent with content on GOV.UK.
  • When we were not sure about a pattern like, gender for children, we contacted Amy Hupe, Senior Content Designer, GOV.UK Design System for help. We also ran the pattern, which she recommended, past David Martin at GEO (David.MARTIN@geo.gov.uk) to check it's compliant from a policy perspective.
  • We designed and iterated content for the alpha prototype to ensure accuracy and clarity through consulting with policy officers and subject matter experts.
  • We reduced the complexity of the old service by removing and grouping questions.
  • We added new questions based on the feedback from user research.

In beta our content designer will consider:

  • Is our service content consistent with content on GOV.UK
  • Is our service content consistent with related services?
  • Will the language we’ve used be relevant to users further down the line, eg gatekeepers?
  • Are there areas that are harder to understand? Can we simplify them or provide helpful guidance?
  • Is there existing related content, for example on GOV.UK, or other communications, that we need to consider? Do we need anything new?
